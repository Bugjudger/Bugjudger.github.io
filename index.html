Papers

Robustness of Deep Learning in Al Assisted Diagnosis: A Comprehensive Study, (in Chinese, final report for project SafeAI)
Chenxu Hu, Yunhao Luo, Shuqi Liu, Tianjun Huang, Jianguo Zhang
Abstract

Medical image analysis is an important and practical AI application. To prevent medical negligence caused by AI, users always place strict requirements for the robustness of medical AI systems. Therefore, it is of great significance to research how AI systems defend against adversarial examples. In this project, we firstly build a unique adversarial example dataset based on International Skin Imaging Collaboration (ISIC) 2018 Dataset. Secondly, we design an online BNN-based defensive training strategy that can significantly increase the model’s accuracy under malicious perturbation. Thirdly, we propose an efficient metric of adversarial robustness called tournament matrix and devise an attention-guided adversarial attack method to generate stealthy and effective. Finally, we have done some preliminary experiments on Visual Transformers. 

Bayesian Attention for Inter-Rater Uncertainty Quantification in Medical Image Segmentation
Qingqiao Hu*, Hao Wang*, Jing Luo, Yunhao Luo, Hongwei Li, Jianguo Zhang
To be submitted to TMI 2021.
Abstract

Semantic segmentation of regions of interest in medical images is inherently ambiguous. For example, the boundary of a structure may not be clearly visible, and different experts may have varying styles or levels of expertise. In this work, we present a simple yet effective framework to estimate inter-rater uncertainty in medical image segmentation tasks. The key idea is to model each rater’s uncertainty in several separated decoders and to introduce probabilistic attention to learn their annotation distributions. First, we demonstrate the motivation of the one-encoder-m-decoder architecture choice compared to the traditional one-encoder-one-decoder one. We further introduce a probabilistic attention gate module to capture the underlying segmentation distribution. We performed extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. Our method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in five tasks (out of seven) under two evaluation metrics considering two aspects of uncertainty modeling.

Threat of adversarial examples on medicalimage analysis: A survey
Chenxu Hu, Yunhao Luo, Shuqi Liu, Tianjun Huang, Zheng Hu, Jianguo Zhang
In progress for submission in early 2022.
Abstract

Recently, deep learning has rapidly developed in medical image classification, denoising, reconstruction, segmentation, and detection. However, there are still many unsettled issues in processing adversarial examples. We review the history of adversarial attacks, analyze the difference between medical and natural images, and introduce defense strategies. This review provides a broad overview of recent literature on medical adversarial examples, discusses shortcomings and opportunities of related methods, and elaborates potential future work.
